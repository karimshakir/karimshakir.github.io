Portfolio

Naturally Curious

As a kid, I've always been curious. I wanted to know how things worked. Be it the family clock, my toys, or the refrigerator, I was always investigating how it worked, and consequently, how I could improve it's current design. Growing up, my school counselors always said that I would be some type of engineer. All I knew was that I liked math, building things and taking things apart.

Alumni

In high school, I wanted to get involved. I played football, ran track, completed internships and did community service. These experiences taught me a lot about working in groups and how individual accomplishments contribute to the success of the team. I applied this knowledge during my tenure as an engineering student at the University of Illinois Urban / Champaign.

Self Actualization

Solving problems, learning new technologies and choosing the best way to create eloquent/engaging solutions for clients are just some of the things I love about developing. It's understanding the nuances of different languages to find the best solution for the client out of endless possibilities. It's developing the finesse to write eloquent code. Actualize helped me to realize that my potential as a programmer has no limits unless I choose to set them. I choose not to.
